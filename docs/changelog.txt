FastSMRW changelog
==================

0.0.3 (in development)
----------------------

- New Invisible interface (Settings > Invisible interface): global hotkeys drive FastSMRW from any window, speaking the post you land on and the timeline and place when you switch (e.g. "Home, 1 of 500").
- New Keyboard Manager (Application menu, or the Invisible interface tab): create, edit, unbind, and share custom keymaps; files are interchangeable with the desktop client.
- Ctrl+Win+W (invisible interface) shows or hides the FastSMRW window from anywhere, and the app remembers whether it was hidden across restarts.
- New Low-level keyhook mode (Invisible interface tab): captures shortcuts the Windows shell reserves - like Win+arrows - that global hotkeys can't grab.
- The Keyboard Manager now includes read-only built-in keymaps shipped with the app (drop .keymap files in the program's keymaps folder); Windows 10 and 8.1 keymaps are included.
- Gaps left when you were away are now remembered and filled in automatically as you scroll down into them, so the timeline stays continuous.
- The scroll position for loading older posts is saved in the cache, so it keeps working after a restart or account switch.
- Timelines now load older posts automatically as you reach the bottom (seamless infinite scroll), in every timeline that supports it.
- Loading older posts keeps working after a restart even when your cache is smaller than what you'd scrolled through, including on Bluesky.
- Bluesky now loads up to 100 posts per request instead of 40, so timelines fill in faster.
- Single-letter shortcuts on a post (R, B, F, Q, E, U) no longer jump your place in the timeline.
- Bluesky now supports the same social features as Mastodon: threads, an author's posts, Followers / Following, and profile with follow / mute / block.
- Fixed the startup "ready" chime playing every time you switched accounts; it now plays only once at startup.
- The app now reopens to the account you were last using.
- Fixed being stuck at the cached number of posts after launching or switching accounts.
- Speech settings now cover posts, users, and notifications separately (Speech tab: Configure Posts / Users / Notifications).
- Notification rows now read who acted, what they did, the related post, and the time, and you can reorder those parts.
- People rows (Followers / Following / People search) now follow your Users speech settings.
- New setting (Timelines tab): "Show mentions in the Notifications timeline".
- The New Timeline dialog (Ctrl+T) can now open a Hashtag timeline, Search Posts, or Search People (Mastodon).
- Search People opens as a people list with the same multi-select and batch follow / mute / block as Followers and Following.


0.0.2
-----

- Open a post author's posts as a timeline: press U on a post, use the Status
  menu's Open User Timeline, or the View Author button in Post info.
- Open a user's profile with Ctrl+U (or the Status menu): their name, handle,
  bio, and follower / following / post counts, with buttons to view their posts,
  follow / unfollow, mute, block, hide or show their boosts, or open their
  profile in your browser. The relationship buttons reflect your current
  relationship and announce the result.
- New setting (Settings > Confirmation): ask before blocking a user. When on,
  blocking from a profile asks first.
- When a post involves more than one person (its author plus anyone it mentions,
  quotes, or boosts), U and Ctrl+U show a menu to choose whose posts or profile
  to open.
- Press Space on a post to open its thread (same as View Thread).
- View an account's Followers or Following list (buttons in the Ctrl+U profile):
  each opens as its own timeline of people.
- On a people list you can select one or more people (multi-select) and press
  Enter to follow, unfollow, mute, unmute, block, or unblock them all at once. U
  and Ctrl+U also work on a person in a list (open their posts or profile).
- Close the current timeline with Backspace, from anywhere in the window.
- Clear a timeline with Ctrl+Delete, or Clear All Timelines with Ctrl+Shift+Delete
  (also in the Timeline menu): empties the loaded posts and caches.
- Closing an opened timeline returns you to the timeline you opened it from,
  instead of whatever was next in the list.
